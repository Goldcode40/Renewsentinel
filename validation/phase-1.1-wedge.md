# RenewSentinel — Phase 1.1 (Wedge Spec)

## Target customer (who exactly)
- Owner / office manager at a 1–10 person HVAC shop in New Hampshire (and similar regulated states), doing residential/light commercial service + installs.
- They handle (or should handle) license renewals, insurance COIs, and “proof” requests from GCs, customers, and inspectors.

## Painful outcome (what they fear/lose)
- “We showed up to a job and got stopped” (inspection fails / permit issues / missing licensed tech for gas/oil scope).
- Fines, rework, and schedule chaos when a credential is expired or the wrong person is assigned.
- Lost revenue + reputation hits when a GC/customer asks for proof (COI/license/cert) and they can’t produce it fast.

## One-sentence wedge promise
- RenewSentinel keeps your HVAC shop compliant (licenses + certs + insurance + permits) so you never lose a job to an expiration—and you can produce proof in minutes.

## Top 10 compliance items (starting narrow; NH HVAC first)
> Note: NH does not have a single “general HVAC contractor license” for all heating/cooling work, but NH does regulate key scopes like fuel gas and oil heat through the Mechanical Safety & Licensing Board. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

1. NH Fuel Gasfitter license (for gas piping/appliance work — scope dependent; tracked per licensed individual)
2. NH Oil Heating Technician license (for oil burner/heating equipment work — scope dependent; tracked per licensed individual)
3. Continuing education / code update hours tied to Mechanical Safety licenses (tracked per year / renewal cycle) :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
4. EPA Section 608 Technician Certification (required federally for techs handling refrigerants) :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
5. General Liability insurance policy + COI (proof on demand; track renewal + named insured / limits)
6. Workers’ compensation policy (if you have employees; proof often requested by GCs)
7. Commercial auto policy (if branded/service vehicles are used for work; proof often requested)
8. Business registration / active entity status (NH SOS / “good standing” style proof when required by counterparties)
9. Permits & inspections per job (town/city mechanical permits where applicable; track permit status + inspection date)
10. “Job-required proof packs” (bundle of the above for a GC/customer: COI + relevant license(s) + EPA 608 roster + permit/inspection receipts)
